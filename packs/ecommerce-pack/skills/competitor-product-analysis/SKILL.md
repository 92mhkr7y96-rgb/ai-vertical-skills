# Skill: competitor-product-analysis

## Purpose

Analyze competitor pages, positioning, selling points, pricing strategy, feedback signals, and opportunities.

## Best for

Ecommerce operators, product selectors, listing writers, and ad buyers comparing a product against competitors.

## Not for

Scraping, fabricating competitor data, legal claims, or copying competitor protected content.

## Input

- Competitor product name
- Competitor title
- Competitor price
- Competitor platform
- Competitor bullet points
- Competitor description
- Competitor review excerpts
- Competitor image notes
- Our product notes

## Output

1. Competitor positioning
2. Target customer assumption
3. Main selling points
4. Pricing strategy
5. Page strengths
6. Page weaknesses
7. Review-based opportunities
8. Differentiation opportunities
9. Our listing improvement suggestions
10. Ad angle opportunities
11. Product image opportunities

## Process

1. Extract only evidence present in the input.
2. Label assumptions separately.
3. Compare competitor strengths and gaps against our product notes.
4. Convert gaps into listing, ad, and image opportunities.
5. Flag risks to avoid.

## Quality Bar

- Does not invent competitor data.
- Separates input-derived observations from assumptions.
- Gives actionable recommendations.
- Identifies risks we can avoid.
- Provides differentiation angles.

## Example Input

Competitor product name: rechargeable camping lantern
Competitor price: $29.99
Competitor review excerpts: users like brightness; some mention unclear charging instructions
Our product notes: includes printed quick-start card

## Example Output

Review-based opportunity: make charging setup more visible in listing copy, image text, and FAQ.

## Safety / Compliance Notes

Do not copy competitor wording. Do not infer private sales data, certifications, or review themes not supplied by the user.
